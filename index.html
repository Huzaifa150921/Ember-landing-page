Our Philosophy

One heat source.
Zero shortcuts.

There is no gas line in our kitchen. Every plate that leaves it — the bread, the vegetables, the fish, the fat rendered for the sauce — has spent time over live coals. It's slower. It's harder to control. It's also the only way we know how to get flavor that a dial can't replicate.

Our chefs read the fire the way a sommelier reads a vintage: by color, by crackle, by the smoke it throws. Three woods, three temperaments, one hearth built into the center of the room so you can watch it happen.
